# Module 01 — Hints

## Checklist for a specific prompt

Before you run a prompt, ask yourself:

- [ ] **Who am I?** Have I told the model my role or relevant background?
- [ ] **What do I want?** Is the task described in one clear sentence?
- [ ] **Who is the audience?** (If the output is for someone to read/use.)
- [ ] **What format should the output be in?** (Paragraph, list, table, JSON, code…)
- [ ] **What length is appropriate?** (One line, a paragraph, 500 words…)
- [ ] **What constraints matter?** (Tone, things to avoid, specific requirements.)
- [ ] **Am I asking one thing or many?** (If many, split into multiple prompts.)

## Common mistakes

**Too open-ended:** "Tell me about machine learning" → add a specific angle and scope.

**Missing audience:** "Explain this to a non-technical person" is better than just "explain this" — even if you know the audience, the model doesn't.

**Assuming shared knowledge:** You know your project, your stack, your company. The model doesn't. Spell it out.

**Forgetting to say what you don't want:** "Don't use jargon", "avoid bullet points", "don't repeat the question back to me" are all valid constraints.
